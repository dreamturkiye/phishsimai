KnowBe4 is a capable platform. It's also built, priced, and sold for the enterprise — annual contracts, per-seat minimums, sales calls, and a feature surface most small teams will never touch. If you're a 15-person company or an MSP running simulations for a handful of client tenants, you've probably felt the mismatch: you're buying a jet to cross the street.

Here's an honest comparison, including where KnowBe4 genuinely wins.

**Where KnowBe4 is the right call**

If you're a large enterprise with a dedicated security team, KnowBe4's depth is real — an enormous template library, extensive training courseware, and mature integrations. If those are your requirements and budget isn't the constraint, it's a defensible choice, and we won't pretend otherwise.

**Where it's overkill**

For small teams and MSPs, the friction usually comes down to three things:

- **Pricing and contracts.** Enterprise SKUs, annual commitments, and per-seat minimums that don't fit a 20-person shop or a per-tenant MSP model.
- **Complexity.** A large console with far more surface than a lean team needs to run a monthly simulation and pull a report.
- **Time-to-value.** Onboarding and configuration overhead when all you need is "send a realistic test, train the clickers, export the log."

**What a leaner alternative should give you**

If your actual job is passing a cyber-insurance renewal, satisfying a compliance line item, or just building genuine awareness, the requirements are modest and concrete:

- Realistic simulations that **reach the inbox** (with clear allowlist guidance for Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace).
- A **training landing page** that turns a click into a teachable moment.
- **Completion and report tracking**, with exportable, timestamped logs.
- **Per-tenant separation** if you're an MSP running multiple clients.
- Month-to-month pricing that scales with what you actually use.

Pick KnowBe4 if you're an enterprise that needs its depth. Pick a lean alternative if you're a small team or MSP that needs the core loop — simulate, train, report — without the contract and the console. Try both if you're unsure; the difference in fit is usually obvious within a week.
